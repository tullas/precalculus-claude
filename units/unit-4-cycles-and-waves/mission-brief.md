# Sector 04 — Cycles and Waves

**Mission:** Prove the transmitter's circle and the receiver's wave are the same signal.

**Key idea:**

- Walk the unit circle counterclockwise from `(1, 0)` by angle `θ` (radians). Your position is `(cos θ, sin θ)` — that's the definition.
- Freeze just the height and unroll it over time — that traces the sine wave. The wave is the circle's shadow.

**Real world:** Cell towers and satellite dishes are physically aimed using this exact angle math. It's also how a piano tuner reads a sound wave's frequency, how a cardiologist reads the repeating pattern in an ECG, and why a seat on a Ferris wheel traces a perfect sine wave as the wheel turns.

**Try it for real:** A Ferris wheel has a 20 m radius and its center sits 22 m above the ground. How high off the ground is a rider after the wheel has turned a quarter-turn (90°) from the bottom?

**Your task:** Drag the angle around the circle. Match the target angle when asked.
