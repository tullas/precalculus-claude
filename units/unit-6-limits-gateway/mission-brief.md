# Sector 06 — Limits: Gateway to Calculus

**Mission:** The sensor throws ERROR at one input. Report what it would say if it weren't broken there.

**Key idea:**

- A limit asks: as the input approaches `a`, what does the output approach? The function doesn't need to be defined at `a`.
- `0/0` means "wrong tool," not "no limit." Factor and cancel to find the real trend.

**Real world:** A speedometer doesn't measure your speed at an exact instant — it measures how far you traveled over a *tiny* interval and reports the limit as that interval shrinks to zero. That's the same reasoning behind instantaneous velocity in physics, the peak concentration a doctor reads off blood-sample data, and the load an engineer finds a cable's tension approaching right at its failure point.

**Try it for real:** A car is at the 60-mile mark at t = 1 hour and the 60.65-mile mark at t = 1.01 hours. Estimate the car's speed at exactly t = 1 hour.

**Your task:** Zoom into the discontinuity from both sides. Estimate the limit, then verify by factoring.
